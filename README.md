# Basket Probabilities?

## How “Deterministic” Funding Math Becomes Uncertain

### Overview

One of the most tempting mistakes in Treasury futures and FIRV analysis is believing that fair value models produce certainty.

The math itself is deterministic.

The market is not.

This presentation explores why CTD baskets, funding relationships, calendar spreads, and futures fair values become increasingly uncertain as liquidity, positioning, narratives, and funding stress begin to dominate pricing.

The deeper lesson is simple:

**Basket probabilities are not a formula. They are a framework for thinking about uncertainty.**

---

## Topics Covered

### Fair Value Is Not Real Life

A common assumption is that a futures contract trading rich or cheap to theoretical fair value represents an obvious opportunity.

In reality:

* Markets are uncertain
* Liquidity arrives in waves
* Narratives change rapidly
* Regimes can persist longer than expected

A rich futures contract is not necessarily wrong.

A cheap futures contract is not necessarily mispriced.

Sometimes the market is discounting a new regime before the model recognizes it.

---

### CTD Switching Is More Than Macroeconomics

Cheapest-to-deliver selection is often presented as a simple funding exercise.

Reality is more complicated.

CTD dynamics are influenced by:

* Funding conditions
* Implied delivery optionality
* Futures speculation
* Retail participation
* Positioning
* Special repo availability
* Dealer balance sheets

The macro story matters.

The plumbing matters too.

---

### Flows Are Narrative Dependent

Can trader flows be modeled?

To some degree.

Can narratives be modeled?

Much harder.

During stressed environments:

* Credit fears create duration demand
* Inflation fears create duration selling
* Energy shocks reshape positioning
* Unexpected data can trigger large liquidity flows

The challenge is not predicting every event.

The challenge is identifying the narrative and the cluster of events capable of validating it.

---

### Repo Stress: The Ultimate Black Swan

Most Treasury futures frameworks depend on functioning funding markets.

When repo becomes stressed:

* Correlations fail
* Funding assumptions fail
* Calendar relationships fail
* Basket models fail

At that point, the game changes.

The focus shifts away from quantitative probabilities and toward understanding:

* Overnight funding conditions
* Federal Reserve actions
* Liquidity facilities
* Market confidence

In extreme repo stress, the most important model becomes the projected policy response.

---

### Where Are The Basket Probabilities?

The uncomfortable answer:

There aren't any perfect ones.

Basket probabilities sit at the intersection of:

* Quantitative modeling
* Market structure
* Funding conditions
* Narrative analysis
* Experience

This is where fixed income becomes both a science and an art.

The best practitioners combine mathematics with an understanding of how markets behave when assumptions stop holding.

---

## Historical Lessons

### COVID Liquidity Crisis

Many senior money market professionals successfully navigated the COVID funding shock because they understood:

* Collateral runs
* Liquidity stress
* Short-duration funding markets
* Likely Federal Reserve responses

Experience became the edge.

---

### Long Duration Bond Selloff

During the recent duration bear market, quantitative practitioners gained an edge through:

* Statistical frameworks
* Stochastic analysis
* Risk management
* Probabilistic exit identification

Models did not eliminate losses.

They helped manage them.

---

## Final Takeaway

Treasury futures pricing is not just about carry.

CTD selection is not just about funding.

Richness and cheapness are not just about macroeconomics.

At some point every model encounters uncertainty.

The trader's job is not to eliminate uncertainty.

The trader's job is to understand which uncertainties matter most in the current regime.

Because in fixed income:

**The math explains the market.**

**The market decides when the math stops working.**
